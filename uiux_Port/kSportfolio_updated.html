What I've designed

Two case studies that solve real user problems — each built on research, clear problem framing, accessibility considerations, and an interactive prototype.

01
UI/UX Case Study
Instagram Reimagined — User Sovereignty
The Problem
Instagram's power-users reported constant anxiety over privacy — seen receipts, active status, and uncontrolled inbox exposure. There was no native way to browse without broadcasting your presence.
Why These Colours & Typography
I mirrored Instagram's familiar dark palette to reduce cognitive switching cost for existing users, while using teal accents to signal the new "safe space" features — a deliberate colour-semantic choice backed by emotional design theory.
Research & Testing
Surveyed 12 frequent Instagram users. Initial design placed Ghost Mode inside Settings — usability testing revealed users expected it one tap from the home screen. Redesigned placement increased task-completion speed by ~40% in follow-up testing.
All screens pass WCAG AA contrast · Biometric states include non-visual fallbacks
Ghost Mode — one-tap hide Seen receipts + Active Now status
Vault — biometric inbox with masked lock screen notifications
Story Viewer Search — find any viewer by name instantly
Shared Assets Calendar — jump to any date's media in 2 taps

02
UI/UX Case Study
Dukaan 2.0 — Hyperlocal Shopping
The Problem
Local kirana shops and vendors within a 2km radius lose visibility to dark-store quick-commerce despite having competitive prices and faster physical proximity. Users defaulted to apps like Blinkit simply due to discoverability.
Why These Colours & Typography
Warm saffron + deep ink palette was chosen to evoke familiarity and trust associated with local bazaars, while maintaining a modern, clean grid to signal reliability — bridging street-market warmth with app-level professionalism.
Research & Testing
Interviewed 8 local vendors and 10 shoppers. Key finding: vendors feared the onboarding complexity more than any feature. Simplified vendor listing to 3 steps with smart defaults — reduced drop-off at registration by ~50% in prototype testing.
Two-sided flow — both user & vendor journeys tested for accessibility
Identified gap where local vendors lose visibility to dark store models
Designed two-sided platform — user discovery + vendor listing flows
